Salt Fish Scones

The classic breakfast of bake and salt fish takes a new form in a soft and buttery scone. The European favorite scone meets Trinidad's beloved salt fish in a combination great for breakfast or as a snack. Instructions

  1. Adjust the oven rack to the lower-middle position and preheat oven to 350°F (177°C).
  2. Boil the salt fish at least two times to remove excess salt, throwing out the water between boils.
  3. Add oil to a medium skillet and heat over medium heat.
  4. Add seasoning and keep stirring until onions become translucent.
  5. Add salt fish along with 3 tsp of lemon juice last. Continue stirring for 3-4 minutes.
  6. Pre-heat oven to 450°F.
  7. Blend or sift together flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and onion powder. Cut in finely shortening or margarine.
  8. Add salt fish filling to dry ingredients and stir lightly with a fork to make a soft dough. Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead 8-10 times.
  9. Divide dough into 2 parts. Roll each into a six circle. Place on a slightly greased baking sheet.
  10. Score each into quarters and bake in oven for 10-12 minutes.
  11. Store in an airtight container up to 2 days in a cool, dry place, or up to 1 week in refrigerator (even longer in freezer).
